Read Chinese anywhere, instantly.

A pop-up dictionary for Chinese that works on web pages, input fields, and a dedicated reader window for visual novels. Hover to translate, hear pronunciation, and export to Anki — all offline.

How it works

Everything you need

Built for learners who read native content — novels, websites, subtitles, and games.

Hover pop-up dictionary

Point at any Chinese word on a page and see pinyin (with tone marks) and definitions from CC-CEDICT.

Reader for visual novels

A separate window that captures Chinese text copied to your clipboard (e.g. via Textractor) and lets you look it up line by line.

Works in input fields

Unlike most extensions, CDIEXT also translates text inside <input> and <textarea> elements.

Audio pronunciation

Hear any word read aloud with your browser's built-in Chinese text-to-speech voice.

One-click Anki export

Send words straight to Anki via AnkiConnect with a configurable deck, note type, and field mapping.

Yours to customize

Tune fonts, colors, and sizes for the pop-up and reader. Pin the pop-up with sticky mode. Everything stays on your device.

How to use

1

On web pages

Move your cursor over Chinese text. A pop-up appears with the reading and meaning. Click the toolbar icon to toggle look-ups on or off.

2

In the reader

Open the reader window. Copy Chinese text (manually or with Textractor) and it is grabbed automatically. Hover a line to translate it.

3

Listen & save

While the pop-up is open, press the audio hotkey to hear the word, or the Anki hotkey to add it to your deck — no need to chase the pop-up with your mouse.

Keyboard shortcuts

Because the pop-up follows your cursor, the audio and Anki actions also have hotkeys that act on the top entry. Hints are shown right inside the pop-up.

Play audio A
Add top entry to Anki D
Close / dismiss pop-up Esc
Toggle sticky mode Alt + Shift + S
Enable / disable look-ups Click the toolbar icon

Anki setup

  1. 1. Install the AnkiConnect add-on in Anki and keep Anki running.
  2. 2. Allow this extension in AnkiConnect: open Tools → Add-ons → AnkiConnect → Config and add the extension to webCorsOriginList (the simplest option is ["*"]), then restart Anki. Without this, AnkiConnect silently blocks the request.
  3. 3. In CDIEXT Options, set your deck name, note type, and the front/back field names (they must match your Anki setup exactly).
  4. 4. Hover a word and press the Anki hotkey (or click + Anki). The card is created instantly.

FAQ

Does CDIEXT send my data anywhere?

No. The dictionary is bundled with the extension and look-ups happen locally. Audio uses your browser's offline voices, and Anki export talks only to Anki on your own machine.

Why doesn't the pop-up appear?

Make sure look-ups are enabled (the toolbar icon shouldn't show "OFF"), and that you are hovering over actual Chinese characters.

The reader isn't grabbing text.

The reader only reads the clipboard while its window is open, and only when the copied text contains Chinese. Use the Pause button to stop grabbing temporarily.

Can I change the colors and fonts?

Yes — open Options to customize the pop-up and reader styles, sticky mode, and hotkeys.
